Meet Amie

I started Star Stuff Counseling to hold space for and accompany clients on their journey in finding their North Star. Helping people find connections with themselves or others is one of the things I love about counseling.


I was adopted as a child and met my bio family later in life; this event made me look at family dynamics and inner child attachment as a source of healing.


Living abroad for two years with my family helped me see life outside of the American bubble and realize how interconnected we, as humans, are. Interconnected neurobiology helped me understand this experience.


Being a high school teacher, and special education teacher, and a person with autoimmune issues helped me learn how to navigate 504, IEPs, work disability, and medical gaslighting. I learned to advocate for and give tools to my clients so they can advocate for themselves and truly feel empowered to tackle what life throws at them.


As a neurodiverse person, I see the world through a different lens. I see ALL people as awe-inspiring, just like the stars in the sky. And see science as a way to explore the world. I see people from the inside out. The heart first because that is where the connection happens.


Life is hard, and complex traumas happen. Figuring out how to navigate relationships, work, grades, family dynamics, and health requires lots of attention when trauma is clouding daily life. Tools such as EMDR, Inner child attachment, somatic movement, interpersonal nuerobiology and narratives are some of the ways to creatively work through life challenges. I see counseling as a unique and creative way to climb obstacles and overcome them.


I am real just like you, I will laugh, cry and I will be human with you during sessions. We are in this together.

We are all human and life happens. I am more than happy to work with you if you need to reschedule, please just communicate with me 48 hours before if you can. If it becomes a problem I will have to charge for $50 for no shows/no communication. Also, because others are trying to seek help too I need to be mindful of time slots for sessions. Five no shows results in a cancelation of services.

I will not share your information with anyone unless you give me written consent. I will not share your information with your insurance company unless you give me written consent. I will not share your information with your family unless you give me written consent. I will not share your information with your school unless you give me written consent.

First session we meet in person or online. I will send out all information and forms to fill out prior to meeting via Simple Practice portal. Most of it are consent forms. I have to have consent to be able to start sessions.(10 min.)


During intake we will talk about what goals you want to achieve during our time together, dont worry these goals can and most likely will change, but it gives us a starting point. I will ask you some get to know yoou type questions and you can ask me questions as well. I like to keep things simple. This is a get to know you phase, it can take several session before you start to talk to me and that is ok. (60 min)


Sessions can get a little heavy at times, you might want to allow yourself 10 min after session to gather your thoughts and feelings before transitioning to your next task. I say this just so you can plan ahead for when sessions are most convenient for you.(50 min)


This is a journey, give yourself time and space to learn and grow.
